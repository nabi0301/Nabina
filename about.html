About Me

I am an aspiring AI and Data Science Professional with growing interests in Artificial Intelligence, large language models (LLMs), and data-driven problem-solving. I bring a unique blend of experience from business, customer service, and technical coursework in computer science.

I'm currently pursuing my B.S. in Computer Science at the University of the People, where I've built strong foundations in data structures, machine learning, AI, networking, and data security. I also hold a Master's in Management, which strengthens my analytical, organizational, and communication skills.

I served as a research intern at the Pacific Northwest National Laboratory (PNNL) through the Department of Energy's SULI Program, where I developed an LLM-enabled tool prototype to visualize IEEE-118 test bus system data and evaluate the benefits of Dynamic Line Rating (DLR) technology in power systems. As I was introduced to the potential of LLMs during this internship, I became motivated to explore them further and gain more hands-on experience in building LLM-driven applications.

I will graduate in June 2026, and I'm currently applying to Graduate programs in Computer Science through the GEM Fellowship, focusing on pathways that combine AI, data science, and advanced computational methods. I am also actively seeking internship opportunities to further strengthen my skills and real-world experience.

I enjoy working with Python, SQL, and data-focused tools, and I'm continuously learning technologies that support data analysis, AI innovation, and automation. My long-term goal is to pursue a career in data analytics and AI, building meaningful, impactful solutions that combine computation and real-world problem-solving.
